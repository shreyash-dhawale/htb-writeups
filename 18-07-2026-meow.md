## What I Learned
 
1. Telnet is an insecure protocol — credentials transmitted in plaintext, easily intercepted
2. Default credentials on network services are one of the most common real-world vulnerabilities
3. Always check for Telnet (port 23) during reconnaissance — still found in embedded devices and legacy systems

## How I Would Detect This in Splunk
 
```spl
index=main sourcetype=syslog
| rex field=_raw "from (?P<src_ip>\d+\.\d+\.\d+\.\d+)"
| search "telnet" OR "port 23"
| eval technique="T1078 - Valid Accounts via Telnet"
| table _time, src_ip, technique
```
 
---
 
## Remediation
 
- Disable Telnet — replace with SSH which encrypts all traffic
- Change all default credentials immediately after device setup
- Block port 23 at the firewall level unless explicitly required
